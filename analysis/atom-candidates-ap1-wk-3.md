### r-ap1-w3-1 — Catecholamine Selection in Shock States

**What students freeze on:** Matching receptor profiles to hemodynamic deficits. Students memorize that "NE is for sepsis" but cannot explain WHY the alpha-1/beta-1 profile matches the pathology. The dose-dependent alpha/beta shift of epinephrine is poorly understood.

#### atom-epi-dose-dependent-shift-1
**Proposed prompt:** Explain why low-dose epinephrine produces vasodilation and increased heart rate while high-dose epinephrine produces vasoconstriction. Identify which receptors dominate at each dose range and describe the clinical implications for anesthesia providers titrating an epinephrine infusion.
**Load-bearing because:** CRITICAL kp2 (weight 1) and kp3 (weight 2) require understanding that the alpha/beta balance shifts with dose; without this, students cannot safely titrate epinephrine.
**Source MCQs:** ap1-w3-001, ap1-w3-002

#### atom-ne-septic-first-line-1
**Proposed prompt:** Explain why norepinephrine is first-line for septic shock by matching its receptor profile (strong alpha-1, moderate beta-1, minimal beta-2) to the specific hemodynamic derangements of septic shock, and explain why phenylephrine and dopamine are inferior choices.
**Load-bearing because:** CRITICAL kp1 (weight 2) requires mechanistic reasoning, not guideline memorization. Students must connect pathologic vasodilation to alpha-1 need and concurrent cardiac dysfunction to beta-1 need.
**Source MCQs:** ap1-w3-007, ap1-w3-008, ap1-w3-015, ap1-w3-025
